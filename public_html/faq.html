General

Do I have to be an expert or have background in the field to join Blockchain at Berkeley?
Absolutely not! Blockchain at Berkeley aims to create a tight network of collaborators and educators in the space with any skill sets and any level of blockchain-related proficiency. Our mission is to educate the general public alongside our exciting and innovative projects.

Cal Students

What makes Blockchain at Berkeley different from other CS developer clubs?
Blockchain at Berkeley focuses on an entirely new space. Our work and community extends beyond the campus, and while we provide opportunities in education and projects (for consultants and developers), what you get out of it is ultimately up to you. Members become experts in niche areas of blockchain with just a few weeks of self-studying; they are now working on noteworthy projects with many of our connections in the industry. We are working on a certification project with several other leading universities and institutions to set the knowledge standard for the space. As pioneers in the blockchain space, we often are building the first blockchain divisions and projects at companies and organizations. We offer opportunities in internal and external research, education, and consulting. If you have questions about recruitment or volunteering, please contact us at recruitment@blockhain.berkeley.edu.
Is Blockchain at Berkeley a campus organization or a non-profit organization run by Cal students?
Both! While we began as an ASUC-sponsored campus organization focused on education, we evolved to offer consulting services to companies from all types of industries. Our non-profit status helps us accomplish our mission beyond the campus.
How can I join Blockchain at Berkeley?
For currently enrolled Cal students of any standing, we recruit every semester for consultants and developers to work on internal and external projects. Catch us on Sproul and by Soda, and follow us online for news on recruitment. We also offer a Blockchain Fundamentals DeCal every semester in addition to events and workshops open to the public.
Do I have to have a technical background to be recruited by or to join Blockchain at Berkeley?
Not at all! Educating members and building experts is the main focus of Blockchain at Berkeley. While a technical background may come in handy for understanding certain topics or for participating in certain projects, we welcome members from all backgrounds!

Cal Alums and Non-Cal Students

Is Blockchain at Berkeley only open to currently enrolled Cal students?
Most Blockchain at Berkeley events are open to anyone who wants to attend. For the sake of maximizing our education’s impact, we try to make all our events and workshops open and accessible to the general public.
I heard about the certification program Blockchain at Berkeley is working on? Is that only open to Cal students?
No, in accordance with our mission of accessible education in the space, we’re hoping to offer this certification program to everyone free of charge, but will be launching from our internal contacts first as a pilot. For more information on release or how to participate, please contact education@blockhain.berkeley.edu.
As a Cal alum or a non-Cal student, how can I get more involved with Blockchain at Berkeley?
Request to join us on Slack (https://berkeleyblockchain.slack.com/) by filling out https://goo.gl/forms/ZwG904RrKBzFsLX03, check out our educational materials on the website, and follow us on social media (Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn) to stay up-to-date with the latest news and opportunities from our org!
I have this interesting proof-of-concept or project I want to explore with the help of Blockchain at Berkeley. How would I go about doing this?
We are always interested in hearing new ideas and working on interesting projects. While projects are usually undertaken with organizational sponsors, we are always open to help with individual collaborators. For individuals interested in collaborating on a project, we ask that they have a thorough proposal/proof-of-concept ready; please check out our website for educational resources if you need help. When you are ready, please send an email to consulting@blockhain.berkeley.edu with the subject line “Consulting: Blockchain Project Proposal” - [your full name] so we can see if our capabilities match with your needs. Please contact the above email for formal proposals. But if you have inquiries or discussion-based and exploration-based questions, we recommend you join our slack channel first to have more specific and lengthy questions and discussions answered. Request to join us on Slack (https://berkeleyblockchain.slack.com/) by filling out https://goo.gl/forms/ZwG904RrKBzFsLX03.

Companies, Government, and Other Entities

How would my company go about working with Blockchain at Berkeley on research or consulting projects?
Our consultancy arm takes on projects on a case-by-case basis, with considerations made based on timeline, nature and scope of project, and potential. For more information, please contact us at consulting@blockhain.berkeley.edu.
Does Blockchain at Berkeley offer educational services or speaking engagements for my company or organization?
Yes, we offer several education programs for companies and employees. For more information, please contact us at education@blockhain.berkeley.edu.
My organization is interested in buying or licensing educational material from Blockchain at Berkeley. How would that work?
If you are interested in licensing our material for third-party use or instruction, please contact us at education@blockhain.berkeley.edu.
My organization is interested in sponsoring your organization. How would that work?
We offer sponsorship opportunities for both Blockchain at Berkeley’s general funds and for our specific projects and events, such as workshops and conferences. For more information, please contact us at sponsorship@blockhain.berkeley.edu.

EVENTS

Where can I stay up-to-date with upcoming events?
Signup for our email newsletter on our website, join our Slack, and follow us on all our social media to get the latest news, events, and opportunities!
Are events open to the public and free?
Our events are open to the public and free unless explicitly stated. However, space limitations may mean attendance caps. For limited or exclusive events, Cal students may be prioritized.
Do I need to register ahead of time?
No, unless explicitly specified. For limited or exclusive events where attendance is limited, registration may occur through Eventbrite or some other medium.
